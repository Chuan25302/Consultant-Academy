# ISO 50001 — Energy Management System Skill Card

## Quick facts
- **What**: Voluntary international standard for Energy Management System (EnMS)
- **Latest version**: ISO 50001:2018
- **Sister standard**: ISO 50002 (audit methodology), ISO 50003-50007 (auditor competence, M&V, EnPI, etc)
- **Compatibility**: aligned with ISO 9001/14001/45001 high-level structure (HLS) → easy to integrate

## Core requirements (PDCA cycle)
1. **Plan** — Energy review, baseline (EnB), Energy Performance Indicators (EnPI)
2. **Do** — Implement action plan, training, communication, control of operations
3. **Check** — Monitor + measure + verify + internal audit
4. **Act** — Management review, continual improvement

## Mandatory elements
- **Top management commitment** (energy policy signed by CEO/MD)
- **Energy review** identifying significant energy uses (SEUs)
- **Baseline (EnB)** + **EnPI** (e.g., kWh/ton product)
- **Action plan** with targets + responsible persons
- **Operational control** of SEUs
- **Documented information** (procedures + records)
- **M&V** of energy performance improvement

## Common Thai context
- กรอ.4 (annual energy report) provides much of the data needed
- ม.32 PRE (Person Responsible for Energy) overlaps with ISO 50001 representative role
- DEDE Energy Audit Type II output → supports SEU identification
- Cost to certify (Thailand): typically 200,000–500,000 บาท for first year (3-yr cycle)

## Common pitfalls in implementation
- EnPI poorly chosen — too aggregate or not normalized for production
- Baseline drift because of process change → no recalibration plan
- Documentation overload — too many SOPs, nobody reads
- Internal audit done by people who don't know energy
- Management review becomes a "tick box" without real action

## Talking to client about ISO 50001
- Costs: certification + ~0.5–1.0 FTE energy manager
- Benefits: typical 5–15% energy reduction in 2 years post-certification
- Often required by MNC parent (especially European, Japanese)
- Can be standalone or co-certified with ISO 14001 for efficiency
