# Hospitals — Skill Card

## Thai context
- Major chains: BDMS (Bangkok Hospital, Samitivej), BCH, Bumrungrad, Vibhavadi, Praram9
- Public: ศิริราช, จุฬา, รามา + provincial hospitals
- Medical hub: international patients drive demand for premium facilities

## Energy profile (typical 200-bed hospital)
- **HVAC**: 50–60% (24/7 + critical zones + humidity + AIIR)
- Hot water + sterilization (autoclave): 10–15%
- Lighting (24/7 areas + emergency): 8–12%
- Medical equipment + IT/imaging (CT, MRI): 10–15%
- Elevators + general: 5–8%

## Critical zones (cannot compromise comfort/safety)
- **OR** (operating room) — positive pressure, HEPA, 20+ ACH
- **ICU/NICU** — temp + RH critical
- **AIIR** (Airborne Infection Isolation Room) — negative pressure, HEPA exhaust
- **Pharmacy compounding** — clean room class
- **Lab** — fume hoods (constant exhaust)

## High-impact opportunities
- Demand-controlled ventilation in non-critical zones (OPD, admin)
- Chiller plant optimization (often oversized for design day, runs at part load)
- Hot water heat pumps (replace some boiler load) + WHR from chiller condenser
- LED retrofit in non-critical areas
- Schedule cleaning + maintenance during off-peak

## Standards
- **ASHRAE 170** — Ventilation of Health Care Facilities
- **JCI** (Joint Commission International) — for international accreditation
- **HA** (สถาบันรับรองคุณภาพสถานพยาบาล / สรพ.) — Thai accreditation
- **WHO GMP** — for hospital pharmacy compounding
- **อาคารควบคุม** ตาม พ.ร.บ. ส่งเสริมการอนุรักษ์พลังงาน 2535

## Talking to this customer
- Facility Manager + Director (often a doctor) + Finance + Infection Control
- Medical staff veto power — never compromise patient safety/comfort
- Long capital cycles; phased projects work better than big-bang
