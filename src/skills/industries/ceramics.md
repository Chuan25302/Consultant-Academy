# Ceramics / Tile — Skill Card

## Thai context
- Major tile: SCG-Cotto, RCI, Dynasty, Sosuco (Saraburi cluster)
- Sanitary ware: SCG-Cotto, American Standard
- Tableware: Royal Porcelain, Suntop
- Refractory + technical ceramics

## Energy profile (typical tile plant)
- **Kiln** (firing): 50–65% of plant energy (gas-fired, peak temp 1100–1250°C)
- **Spray dryer** (body preparation): 15–25% (hot gas + atomization)
- **Dryers** (between forming and firing): 10–15%
- **Mills** (raw + glaze): 5–10% (electricity)
- **Compressed air + general**: 5–10%

## Kiln specifics
- **Roller kiln** most common for tile (continuous, faster than tunnel kiln)
- **Shuttle kiln** for sanitary ware (batch, more flexible)
- Heat balance: typically only 30–40% goes into product; rest is losses
- Cool zone heat → recoverable for dryers

## High-impact opportunities
- **Cool-zone heat recovery** to spray dryer + body dryer (10–25% energy savings)
- **Kiln insulation upgrade** + crown maintenance
- **Burner tuning + O₂ trim** (excess air = lost heat)
- **Spray dryer optimization** — slip moisture content reduction
- **Mill loading optimization** — under/over-charging both waste energy
- **LED + high-bay lighting** in production halls
- **Compressed air leak program** + reduced pressure setpoint

## Standards
- **ISO 13006** — ceramic tile (definitions + classification)
- **ISO 50001** — for large operators
- **มอก.** specific TIS for tile + sanitary ware
- **EHIA** for kiln emissions in Thailand (NOx, SOx, particulates)

## Sustainability angle
- **Embodied carbon of tile** — material selection + clay type matters
- **Alternative fuel** (biomass, RDF from MSW) for kiln — emerging in cement; ceramics next
- **Closed-loop water + sludge recovery**
- **Product carbon footprint** (ISO 14067) for export to EU markets
