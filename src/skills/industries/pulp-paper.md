# Pulp & Paper — Skill Card

## Thai context
- Major: SCG Packaging, Double A, Phoenix Pulp & Paper, Thai Paper Industry
- Cluster: Kanchanaburi, Prachinburi, Saraburi (near eucalyptus + bagasse sources)
- Containerboard + tissue + writing paper segments

## Energy profile (typical kraft mill)
- **Steam**: 60–70% of plant energy (digester, dryer, evaporator)
  - Most mills run their own **biomass/black-liquor recovery boiler**
- **Electricity**: 25–35% (refiners, pumps, fans, vacuum systems, paper machine)
- **Water heating + chilling**: 5–10%
- Self-generated power (cogen) often offsets 50–80% of grid demand

## Common energy hot spots
- **Paper machine dryer section** — biggest single steam user (~50–70% of steam)
- **Refiners** — major motor load (electricity-heavy step)
- **Vacuum systems** — often oversized; running multiple pumps unnecessarily
- **Compressed air** — for instrumentation + sheet handling (leak-prone)
- **Wastewater treatment** — aerator + pump load

## High-impact opportunities
- **Steam trap survey** — typical 10–20% of traps fail open in pulp mills
- **Drying optimization** — IR/condensate removal + felt cleanliness
- **VSD on refiners and pumps** with variable load
- **Black liquor concentration** improvement (more solids = less evaporator load)
- **Heat recovery** from white water + dryer exhaust
- **Compressed air leak program** + pressure drop reduction

## Standards
- **TAPPI** (Technical Association of the Pulp & Paper Industry)
- **ISO 50001** — common in larger mills
- **FSC / PEFC** — forest certification (sustainability reporting upstream)
- **ASME I/IV** — recovery boilers + power boilers

## Sustainability angle
- **Biomass boiler** + **black liquor recovery** = renewable fuel base
- T-VER project potential (especially biomass + biogas from wastewater)
- Carbon-negative possible if forest source verified + cogen export
