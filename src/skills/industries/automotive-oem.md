# Automotive OEM (Full Vehicle) — Skill Card

## Thai context
- Thailand = "Detroit of Asia": Toyota, Honda, Isuzu, Mitsubishi, Mazda, Nissan, MG, BYD
- Pickup truck capital — Hilux, D-Max, Triton manufactured here
- EV transition: BOI EV3.0/EV3.5 incentives, BYD/MG/Neta plants ramping
- Common location: Eastern Seaboard (Laem Chabang, Amata, Hemaraj)

## Energy profile (typical OEM plant)
- **Paint shop**: 30–45% of plant energy (oven, booth makeup air, regenerative thermal oxidizer)
- Body weld shop: 15–25% (welding robots, dust collection)
- Stamping: 10–15% (presses, scrap conveyor)
- Compressed air system: 10–15% (massive — pneumatic everywhere)
- General lighting + HVAC: 10–20%

## High-impact opportunities
- Paint oven heat recovery → preheat make-up air (5–15% paint shop savings)
- VSD on stamping press hydraulic pumps (30–50% press energy)
- Compressed air leak program + sequencer
- LED retrofit in body shop
- Off-shift shutdown protocol (often production stops but utilities don't)

## Standards mandatory in this sector
- **IATF 16949** — automotive QMS (energy + utilities tracked here)
- **ISO 14001 + 45001** — most OEMs require both
- **VDA 6.3** — process audit
- **EHIA** — for paint shop emissions in Thailand

## Talking to this customer
- Engineering Manager + Facility Manager + ESG Officer (3 roles)
- They report energy KPIs to global parent monthly
- Capital approval often goes through Japan/Detroit/Munich HQ
- Scope 3 pressure from OEMs is real — supplier code requires energy disclosure
