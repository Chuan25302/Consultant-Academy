# Rubber / Glove / Tire — Skill Card

## Thai context
- Thailand = #1 natural rubber (NR) producer globally + major glove + tire hub
- Glove: Sri Trang Gloves, Ansell, post-COVID capacity expansion
- Tire OEM: Bridgestone, Michelin, Yokohama, Goodyear, Continental
- Block rubber + RSS exporters: Sri Trang, Vongbandit, Tongthai

## Energy profile (typical glove plant)
- **Drying ovens** (post-dipping, post-leaching, vulcanization): 40–55% of energy
- **Steam** (wash, leaching, autoclave): 20–30%
- **Compressed air** (former cleaning, transport): 10–15%
- **Cooling** (chiller + cooling tower for compounds + chlorination): 10–15%

## Tire plant differences vs glove
- **Curing presses** (vulcanization with steam + pressure): largest energy block
- **Mixers** (Banbury): high power short bursts
- **Extruders**, calenders for tread + sidewall

## High-impact opportunities (glove + tire)
- **Heat recovery from vulcanization/curing** — massive waste heat stream
- **Steam system audit** — trap survey, insulation, condensate return
- **Oven exhaust energy recovery** (tunnel oven heat exchangers)
- **Compressed air leak program** (often 25–40% leakage in older plants)
- **VSD on chiller pumps + cooling tower fans**
- **Right-sizing dryer fans** (often oversized for capacity ramp-up that never came)

## Standards
- **ISO 9001** + **IATF 16949** (tire OEM supplier requirement)
- **ISO 14001 + 45001** common in larger plants
- **FSC / PEFC** for natural rubber traceability (EUDR coming)
- **มอก.** specific TIS for medical glove + tire

## Sustainability angle
- **EUDR** (EU Deforestation Regulation) — affecting NR exporters from 2025
- **Scope 3** pressure from tire OEMs to suppliers
- **Used tire recycling** — pyrolysis + carbon black recovery emerging
