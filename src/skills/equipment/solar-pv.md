# Solar PV (Industrial / Commercial Rooftop) — Skill Card

## Key concepts
- **Capacity factor** in Thailand: 16–19% (annual average, dependent on tilt + shading + soiling)
- **PR** (Performance Ratio) = actual yield ÷ theoretical yield (good system: 0.78–0.85)
- **Specific yield**: 1,400–1,650 kWh/kWp/yr typical for Thailand
- **Tilt + azimuth**: 10–15° south-facing optimal in Thailand (vs 20–30° in cooler climates)
- Module types: mono-PERC (most common), TOPCon (newer high-eff), bifacial (ground/carport)

## Thai context
- **Self-consumption** (no export) is most common — avoids ERC license + utility bureaucracy
- **Direct PPA** (developer owns, customer buys) — hands-off but higher LCOE
- **Net metering** not yet broadly available; net billing pilots running
- BOI exemption for energy projects (incl. PV) — significant tax benefit
- Common installation: 100 kWp – 5 MWp rooftop on factory/warehouse

## Sizing rules of thumb
- 1 kWp ≈ 6–7 m² of usable roof area (mono-PERC, 2024)
- Sizing rule: cover 60–80% of daytime baseload (avoid export complications)
- For 24/7 plant: PV alone displaces 10–25% of total electricity
- For day-shift only: PV can displace 40–60% of electricity

## Common pitfalls
- Roof structural assessment missed → reinforcement adds cost
- Cable losses + inverter sizing mismatch (oversize inverter for cloudy days = loss)
- Soiling not in O&M plan (Thailand dust + bird → 5–15% loss/yr if not cleaned)
- Insurance + lightning protection often forgotten
- PPA contract: who pays for repair after Yr 10? read fine print
