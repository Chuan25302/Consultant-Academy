# Heat Pump (Industrial / Commercial) — Skill Card

## Key concepts
- **COP** = heat output ÷ electrical input (typical 2.5–5+ depending on temp lift)
- **Lift** = sink temp − source temp (smaller lift = higher COP)
- Refrigerants: R134a, R1234ze, R744 (CO₂, transcritical), R290 (propane), NH₃
- Types: air-source, water-source, ground-source (GSHP), high-temp industrial (up to 165°C)

## Why it's a Thai opportunity
- Replace gas/LPG/diesel boilers for hot water + low-pressure steam (≤120°C)
- Combine with solar PV → Scope 1 → Scope 2 → 0 (with REC)
- BOI incentive for energy-efficient retrofit projects

## Typical applications
- Hospital hot water (50–60°C) — replace electric/gas boiler
- Hotel hot water + pool heating
- Food processing wash water (60–80°C)
- Pharma WFI pre-heat (70°C+)
- Drying ovens (low-temp tunnel) — 60–90°C

## Sizing rules of thumb
- Hot water 60°C: COP ~3.5–4.5 typical with air-source
- Process heat 90°C: COP ~2.5–3.5
- High-temp industrial 120–165°C: COP ~1.8–2.5 (still beats gas in many cases)
- Cooling + heating simultaneous (food/dairy) → "heat recovery chiller" often best
