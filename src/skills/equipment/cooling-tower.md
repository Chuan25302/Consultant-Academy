# Cooling Tower — Skill Card

## Key concepts
- **Approach**: water out − ambient wet-bulb (smaller = better, target 3–5°C)
- **Range**: water in − water out (set by process load)
- **Drift**: water droplets carried away — measured % of circulating water
- **Cycles of concentration (COC)**: how concentrated blowdown gets — higher = less makeup water but more scaling risk
- Types: induced-draft cross-flow, induced-draft counter-flow, natural-draft (large utilities)

## Common issues in Thai factories
- **Fouled fill** — biofilm + minerals reduce heat transfer (5–15% efficiency loss)
- **Drift eliminator missing/torn** — water + chemical loss, plus regulatory risk
- **Fan running at full speed** during low-load → wasted power
- **High approach** (>8°C) → upstream chiller sees high condenser temp → COP tanks
- **Make-up water + chemicals over-treated** for current cycles

## Retrofit options + ROI
- VFD on fans (cycle by load): 1–2 ปี — biggest single win
- Replace plastic fill if fouled: 1–3 ปี
- Drift eliminator replacement: 6–12 เดือน + compliance
- Side-stream filtration: 1–2 ปี
- Optimize COC + chemical program: months, no capex

## Standards
- **CTI** (Cooling Technology Institute) — performance certification
- **ASHRAE 90.1** — minimum efficiency in buildings
- **EHIA** in Thailand if drift exceeds emission criteria
- **กฎกระทรวงสิ่งแวดล้อม** — Legionella + drift
