# Chiller — Skill Card

## Key concepts
- **COP** (Coefficient of Performance) = cooling output ÷ electrical input
- **IPLV/NPLV** (Integrated Part-Load Value) — more realistic than full-load efficiency
- **Approach temp** (cooling tower water out − ambient wet bulb), **range** (water in − out)
- **Head pressure** — high condenser pressure kills COP
- Refrigerants: R134a, R410A, R1234ze (low-GWP), R513A — phase-out timelines per Kigali

## Common issues in Thai factories
- Condenser fouling (cooling water minerals + biofilm) — typical 5–15% efficiency loss
- Oversized chiller running at 30–50% load → poor part-load COP
- Setpoint too low (every 1°C lower → ~2–3% more energy)
- Old screw chiller (COP ~3) vs new magnetic-bearing centrifugal (COP 6–7+)

## Retrofit options + typical ROI range
- Tube cleaning + water treatment review: payback 2–6 เดือน
- VFD on cooling tower fan + condenser water pump: 1–2 ปี
- Set point reset + tower water optimization: ~3–8% savings, no capex
- Replace with high-COP unit: 3–7 ปี (load-dependent)

## Standards
- **TIS 2854** — chiller efficiency (Thailand)
- **ASHRAE 90.1** — minimum efficiency
- **AHRI 550/590** — performance rating
