# Pump — Skill Card

## Key concepts
- **BEP** (Best Efficiency Point) — operate within 70–110% of BEP for longest life + best efficiency
- **NPSHa vs NPSHr** — Net Positive Suction Head; cavitation if NPSHa < NPSHr
- **Affinity laws**: Flow ∝ N, Head ∝ N², Power ∝ N³ — small RPM cut = large power saving
- **System curve vs pump curve** — operating point at intersection
- Centrifugal vs positive displacement vs axial

## Common issues in Thai factories
- Throttling discharge valve to control flow → wasted ΔP (use VFD instead)
- Running multiple pumps in parallel always-on (sequence by demand instead)
- Cavitation damaging impeller (low NPSHa from old strainers)
- Wrong impeller diameter (pump originally sized for "future load")

## Retrofit options + ROI
- VFD instead of throttling: 20–50% savings on variable flow
- Trim impeller to match actual head: 5–15% savings
- Stop unnecessary parallel pump (sequence): 10–25% savings
- High-efficiency replacement: 2–4 ปี payback
